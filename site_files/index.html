Fashioning Uganda

20 Years of Style in Uganda's Pearl

A study of Uganda's beauty and fashion industries through a global-intimate lens


Welcome! This web archive contains 6000 images of New Vision Magazine, published in Uganda in the 90s and 00s. Dr. Caroline Faria from the Department of Geography and the Environment at UT uses these images as data for her NSF project on the beauty and fashion industry in Uganda. She has made these images available on this web site to university students and faculty for their own research. Feel free to browse the web archive for images, or check explore the site to learn more about Dr. Faria, her research, and UT's Feminist Geography Collective.

About our work...

  • Dr. Caroline Faria
  • Research
  • Feminist Geography Collective

  • Dr. Faria is a feminist geographer and Assistant Professor in the Department of Geography and the Environment at UT Austin. She draws on feminist, critical race, and postcolonial perspectives to interrogate contemporary workings of nationalism and cosmopolitanism. Her current research, funded by the National Science Foundation, examines the political-economies of the beauty industry in the Gulf-East African region.

  • Our research centers around the theme of globalization and commodity flows related to Dr. Faria's NSF-funded research on globalization in the Gulf-East African region. This project focuses on the hair and beauty industry connecting East Africa and the Gulf, and in particular Uganda and Dubai, UAE.   • Our collective brings together faculty, graduate and undergraduate students from within and beyond Geography conducting research on power and place. Our research centers the politics of globalization and commodity flows. Our broader activist goal is to foster healthy, vibrant, and supportive academic spaces for feminist geographic research, to support the scholarship of women and minority students, and to connect to geographic, scholarly, and activist communities working globally.
